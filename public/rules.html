üé® Mandala

A strategic two-player card game

üéØ Goal

Score the most points by collecting cards into your Cup and building your River (which determines how much each color is worth).

üÉè Components

The game uses 108 cards in 6 colors:

  • Red (18 cards)
  • Orange (18 cards)
  • Yellow (18 cards)
  • Green (18 cards)
  • Purple (18 cards)
  • Black (18 cards)

There are two Mandalas (game boards), each with a Mountain (center) and two Fields (one per player).

üìã Setup

  • Each player draws 6 cards to form their hand
  • Each player gets 2 hidden cards in their Cup (starting cards)
  • The Mountains and Fields start empty

üéÆ On Your Turn

Do ONE of the following actions:

1. Play to the Mountain

Play 1 card of any color to a Mountain's center.

Restriction: The color cannot already be in either player's Field of that Mandala.

2. Play to Your Field

Play 1 or more cards of the same color to your Field.

Restrictions:

  • The color cannot be in the Mountain of that Mandala
  • The color cannot be in your opponent's Field of that Mandala
  • You must keep at least 1 card in your hand

3. Discard & Draw

Discard 1 or more cards of the same color, then draw cards equal to the number discarded (if available).

üèîÔ∏è Completing a Mandala

When a Mandala has all 6 colors represented (between Mountain and both Fields), it is destroyed!

The Destruction Process:

  1. The player with more cards in their Field claims first (ties: whoever played there first)
  2. Players alternate claiming colors from the Mountain
  3. For each color claimed:
    • 1 card goes to your River (if that color slot is empty)
    • Rest go to your Cup (for end-game scoring)
  4. After all Mountain colors are claimed, Field cards are discarded

üåä The River

Your River has 6 slots numbered 1-6. The first color you add goes in slot 1, second in slot 2, etc.

At game end, the slot number = points per card of that color in your Cup.

üí° Strategy Tip

Get your best colors early in your River (higher numbers = more points). But remember: you only add a color to your River the first time you claim it!

üèÅ Game End

The game ends when a player cannot draw up to 6 cards at the end of their turn (deck is empty).

Scoring:

For each color in your River: River Value √ó Cards in Cup

Example: If Purple is in your River slot 5, and you have 7 Purple cards in your Cup = 5 √ó 7 = 35 points

Highest total score wins!

üí° Quick Tips

  • Claim order matters - Try to be first claimer to get your preferred colors
  • Watch the discard pile - Track which colors are running low
  • River positioning is key - Colors you'll collect many of should be in high-value River slots
  • Block opponents - Play colors to Mountains that your opponent needs in their Field
  • Timing the end - If you're ahead, try to end the game; if behind, keep it going
